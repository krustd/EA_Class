## III. Background of the Selected Business

Luckin Coffee Inc. was founded in 2017 and is headquartered in Xiamen, Fujian, China. Measured by store count, it has grown into one of China's largest coffee chains. Unlike traditional cafe chains that emphasize dine-in experiences, Luckin built its business around a technology-driven "new retail" model: customers order primarily through the Luckin app or WeChat mini-program, choose between pickup and delivery, pay digitally, and receive real-time order updates. This model cuts queuing time, boosts store efficiency, and enables the company to collect customer data that feeds into product development, store operations, marketing, and supply chain planning.

Luckin's core products are freshly brewed drinks, especially coffee and tea-based beverages. Its menu includes Americano, latte, specialty coffee, fruit-flavored coffee, light milk tea, snacks, light meals, and packaged coffee products such as instant coffee, coffee beans, drip coffee bags, capsules, and accessories. The company emphasizes high quality, affordability, and convenience, aligning with the daily consumption habits of office workers, students, young consumers, and price-sensitive mass-market customers.

The company's mission is "to create lucky moments and inspire." Its vision is "to build a world-class coffee brand and become a part of everyone's daily life." These statements reflect its strategy: Luckin does not merely sell coffee as a premium cafe experience; it seeks to make coffee a frequent, convenient, and affordable daily habit.

By the end of 2025, Luckin had 31,048 stores, including 20,234 self-operated stores and 10,814 partnership stores. In 2025, it added 8,708 net new stores and reported RMB49.3 billion in total net revenue, a 43.0% year-on-year increase. Its monthly average transacting customers reached 94.2 million for the full year, underscoring the scale of its digital customer base. The company has also expanded into overseas markets, including Singapore, Malaysia, and the United States, though China remains its primary market.

## IV. Business Analysis

### a. Stakeholders / Key Partners

Luckin's key stakeholders include customers, employees, store managers, partnership store operators, suppliers, logistics partners, technology service providers, payment platforms, delivery platforms, landlords, investors, regulators, and local communities. Among these, suppliers and logistics partners are especially critical, as Luckin relies on consistent ingredients, standardized recipes, and fast delivery to maintain quality across tens of thousands of stores.

The company sources coffee beans from origins such as Brazil, Colombia, Ethiopia, Honduras, Tanzania, and Yunnan, China. It has invested in roasting and supply chain facilities in locations such as Fujian, Jiangsu, and Yunnan, and also works with third-party warehouse and fulfillment providers. This indicates that Luckin's enterprise architecture relies heavily on integrated supply chain systems, warehouse management, order management, and store-level operational data.

### b. Customers: Types, Relationships, Channels

Luckin serves mass-market coffee and beverage consumers. Its main customer groups include office workers who need fast pickup, students and young consumers drawn by affordable drinks and promotions, commuters who opt for delivery or pickup, and corporate clients that use company accounts for employees.

The customer relationship is highly digital and data-driven. Customers interact with Luckin mainly through mobile apps, WeChat mini-programs, third-party delivery platforms, and social media channels. The app supports store recommendation, ordering, payment, pickup or delivery selection, order tracking, and customer feedback. This creates a direct relationship with customers and enables Luckin to leverage order history, preferences, vouchers, and promotions to drive repeat purchases.

### c. Financial Perspective

Luckin generates revenue primarily through product sales from self-operated stores and income from partnership stores. Product sales include freshly brewed drinks, other food and beverage items, delivery-related income, e-commerce, and packaged coffee products. Partnership store revenue includes sales of materials and equipment, delivery service fees, profit sharing, royalty fees, franchise fees, and other services.

In 2025, total net revenue reached RMB49.3 billion. Self-operated store revenue was RMB36.2 billion, while partnership store revenue was RMB11.6 billion. This reflects a hybrid business model: self-operated stores give Luckin greater control over brand, service quality, and data, while partnership stores enable rapid expansion with lower direct capital pressure.

Luckin's main costs include raw materials, store rent, labor, utilities, delivery expenses, depreciation, sales and marketing, general administration, and research and development. The company continues to invest in store expansion, discounts, product innovation, supply chain facilities, technology systems, and marketing campaigns. Its 2025 GAAP operating income was RMB5.1 billion, with an operating margin of 10.3%, indicating that the company has moved beyond pure growth spending into a more profitable, at-scale model.

### d. Internal Business Process

Luckin's main internal processes include product development, procurement, roasting and supply chain management, digital ordering, store operations, delivery coordination, customer service, and data analysis.

The basic process is:

1. The product team develops drinks based on market trends and customer data.
2. Ingredients and coffee beans are sourced, roasted, stored, and distributed through warehouses.
3. Customers place orders through digital channels.
4. The system assigns orders to suitable stores based on location, inventory, and capacity.
5. Store staff prepare drinks using standardized recipes and equipment.
6. Customers pick up orders or receive delivery.
7. Customer feedback and transaction data flow back into operations, marketing, and product development.

From an enterprise architecture perspective, Luckin is a compelling case because its business strategy, data systems, mobile applications, warehouse systems, payment systems, delivery coordination, and store operations are tightly interconnected.

### e. Learning and Growth

Luckin's learning and growth capability stems from rapid product innovation, digital customer insights, standardized training, supply chain improvement, and sustained technology investment. The company has launched numerous new drink SKUs in recent years, using popular products to generate traffic and brand attention. It also invests in research and development, app functionality, warehouse management, and operational systems.

The company is well-positioned to improve because its digital model creates fast feedback loops: sales data reveals which products resonate, customer preferences enable personalized marketing, delivery and pickup data inform store allocation, and store performance data supports expansion decisions. However, Luckin also faces challenges: intense price competition, high delivery costs, quality control across a vast store network, international expansion risk, and the ongoing need to maintain trust after its 2020 accounting scandal.

## Short Presentation Version

Our selected business is Luckin Coffee. Founded in 2017 in Xiamen, China, Luckin has become one of China's largest coffee chains. Its mission is to create lucky moments and inspire, and its vision is to build a world-class coffee brand that becomes part of everyone's daily life.

Luckin's products include freshly brewed coffee, tea drinks, fruit-flavored coffee, light milk tea, snacks, light meals, and packaged coffee products. What sets it apart from traditional coffee chains is its technology-driven new retail model. Customers normally order through the app or mini-program, pay digitally, and choose pickup or delivery. This makes the service fast and convenient, while also generating valuable data for marketing, product development, and operations.

By the end of 2025, Luckin had 31,048 stores, comprising both self-operated and partnership stores. Its 2025 total net revenue was RMB49.3 billion, up 43.0% year on year. This scale demonstrates that Luckin's strategy is not only about selling coffee, but about building a digital, standardized, and efficient beverage platform.

In terms of business analysis, Luckin's key stakeholders include customers, suppliers, logistics partners, franchise/partnership store operators, employees, investors, payment platforms, delivery platforms, and regulators. Its customers are predominantly mass-market consumers such as office workers, students, commuters, young consumers, and corporate clients. Financially, it earns revenue from self-operated store product sales and from partnership stores through materials, equipment, delivery services, profit sharing, royalties, and franchise services. Internally, its core processes connect product development, supply chain, app ordering, store operations, delivery, customer service, and data analysis. For learning and growth, Luckin relies on product innovation, digital feedback, employee training, supply chain investment, and technology systems.

In conclusion, Luckin Coffee is a strong case for enterprise architecture because its competitive advantage hinges on the alignment between business strategy, customer channels, data systems, supply chain, and store operations.

Luckin Coffee is well-suited for this project because its business model connects digital ordering, store operations, customer data, supply chain management, and financial performance. It therefore provides ample material for subsequent analysis of enterprise architecture and database design.
